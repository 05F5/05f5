In the software industry, growth often doesn't mean match with profitability.
Open source companies face an even harder decision : how do you scale adoption, invest in product, and remain financially disciplined ?
This talk is not about financial theory. It is about decisions. The uncomfortable ones
Through concrete examples, I will share the structural choices, internal debates, and governance realities behind building a profitable open source business at scale.
Key themes include:
- The structural differences between open-source economics and traditional SaaS
- Why revenue growth alone is a dangerous metrics
- The evolving role of the CFO in founder-led organizations
- How do you create financial rigor in a culture that values product above all else
